The Host / MC (Role & Style)

**Role:** The voice of the event. Manages the timeline, introduces speakers, engages the audience, and keeps the energy high during transitions.

**Selection:** Choose a Host whose style (**formal, witty, high-energy**) matches your event theme.

DJ Console & Equipment

**Setup:** Requires a sturdy, dedicated table/platform near the dance floor. Must have reliable **power access** separate from lighting.

**Gear:** Professional **DJ Controller/Mixer, Headphones**, and a backup audio source (e.g., secondary laptop).

MC Script & Cue Sheet

**Requirement:** The Host must have a final, approved script with **precise timing, speaker names (pronunciation check!)**, and transitions.

**Critical:** Include **mic checks** and **countdown cues** for technical team coordination.

DJ Playlist & Genre Planning

**Input:** Provide the DJ with a **'Must-Play' list, a 'Do-Not-Play' list,** and the preferred music genres for different phases (e.g., Dinner/Cocktail/Dance).

Host & DJ Coordination (Key)

**Collaboration:** The Host and DJ must coordinate live. The Host signals for entry music, exit music, or a change in lighting/vibe directly to the DJ.

**Tools:** Use a **dedicated communication channel** (e.g., direct line via intercom/walkie-talkie) between the two.

Host Microphone & Sound Check

**Gear:** Provide the Host with a high-quality **Wireless Headset or Handheld Microphone** (preferred). Always have a fully charged backup microphone ready.

**Test:** A full sound check is mandatory one hour before guests arrive to set the correct gain/volume.

Vibe Check & Audience Reading

**Skill:** A good DJ/Host can read the crowd's energy and adjust the music tempo or interaction style immediately.

**Flexibility:** They should be flexible enough to handle spontaneous requests or make on-the-spot changes to the script/playlist if needed.

Technical Rider & Venue Check

**Requirement:** The DJ should submit a **Tech Rider** (list of required equipment, power, and space) beforehand.

**Venue Team:** The venue's sound engineer or event manager must verify that all technical requirements can be met.
