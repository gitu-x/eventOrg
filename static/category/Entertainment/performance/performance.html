Live Performance & Artist Management

Managing the logistics, technical needs, and comfort of live acts, bands, and performers for a stellar show.

Artist Selection & Type

**Variety:** Determine the type of act (Live Band, Classical Dancer, Stand-up Comedian, Magician) that best suits the event theme and audience demographic.

**Contract:** Finalize the performance date, time, duration, and **cancellation clause** in the agreement.

Technical & Hospitality Rider

**Crucial:** The artist's **Tech Rider** lists all required sound/light equipment (mics, monitors, instruments). The **Hospitality Rider** details food, water, transportation, and dressing room needs.

**Compliance:** Ensure the venue can meet all specified requirements before finalizing the booking.

Stage Plot & Staging Layout

**Plan:** A diagram showing the exact placement of every musician, instrument, monitor speaker, and microphone on the stage.

**Logistics:** This plan is vital for the technical team to complete stage setup (patching) efficiently and correctly.

Sound Check & Monitor Mix

**Timing:** Schedule a dedicated **sound check session** well before doors open. This is non-negotiable for live acts.

**Monitor Mix:** The artist needs a custom **Monitor Mix** (what they hear on stage) to perform effectively. This is managed by the Sound Engineer.

Dressing Room & Green Room

**Comfort:** A clean, private, climate-controlled space (Green Room) for the artists to relax, change, and prepare.

**Amenities:** Must include private restroom access, basic furniture, mirrors, and items requested in the Hospitality Rider.

Rehearsal & Entrance Timing

**Run-Through:** If the act involves the MC or background dancers, a brief rehearsal is necessary to confirm **stage entries, exits, and lighting cues**.

**Cue Time:** Clearly communicate the exact **"hold" and "go" times** to the stage manager and the artist liaison.

Fees, Advances & Payments

**Finance:** Clearly define the payment structure (e.g., 50% advance, 50% post-performance). Ensure the final payment is ready **on-site** if required by the contract.

**Taxes:** Clarify responsibility for performance taxes, service tax, or any required withholding.

Performance Scheduling & Run of Show

**Flow:** Integrate the performance seamlessly into the overall event run-of-show. Avoid placing key acts immediately after long speeches or during dinner service.

**Duration:** Stick strictly to the contracted performance duration to avoid disrupting later acts or the venue's curfew.
