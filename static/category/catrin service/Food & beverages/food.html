Buffet Service (Self-Serve)

**Style:** Guests move to dedicated food stations. Offers **variety and quick service** for large crowds. Requires larger floor space.

**Pros/Cons:** Cost-effective, but requires careful temperature monitoring and constant restocking.

Plated / A La Carte Service

**Style:** Food is pre-portioned and served directly to seated guests. Offers the **most elegant and formal** experience.

**Pros/Cons:** High per-person cost, requires a high staff-to-guest ratio, but is highly structured and minimizes waste.

Interactive Food Stations

**Style:** Specialized stations (e.g., Live Pasta Bar, Taco Stand, Dessert Counter) where food is cooked or prepared **fresh on demand**.

**Benefit:** Adds a fun, customizable, and interactive element to the dining experience.

Beverage & Bar Service

**Options:** Choose between **Cash Bar** (guests pay), **Open Bar** (host pays), or **Limited Bar** (beer/wine only). Ensure adequate licensed bartenders.

**Non-Alc:** Always provide attractive non-alcoholic options (mocktails, fresh juices) beyond basic soda/water.

Dietary Restrictions & Allergies

**Critical:** Collect **RSVP information** regarding allergies (e.g., nuts, gluten), vegetarian, vegan, or religious meal requirements well in advance.

**Safety:** Ensure the kitchen and serving staff clearly label and handle special meals separately to prevent cross-contamination.

Catering Staffing & Ratio

**Calculation:** Ensure the ratio of servers to guests is adequate. **Plated service** needs 1 server per 10 guests. **Buffet** needs 1 server per 20-30 guests.

**Logistics:** Confirm setup, serving, and teardown times with the catering manager.

Food Timing & Event Flow

**Planning:** Food service must align perfectly with the event timeline. Avoid serving the main course during key speeches or performances.

**Breaks:** Plan coffee/tea breaks and cocktail hours to manage guest flow and allow time for venue transitions.

Menu Tasting & Finalization

**Review:** Conduct a **menu tasting session** 4-6 weeks before the event. Use this to finalize dishes, presentation, and make necessary spice/flavor adjustments.

**Contract:** The final contract should clearly list all agreed-upon dishes, portion sizes, and service timeline.
