Plates & Bowls (The Crockery)

**Variety:** Dinner plates (main course), Salad/Appetizer plates, Bread plates, and Dessert bowls. Choose material (**Porcelain** for elegance, **Ceramic** for rustic).

**Requirement:** Must be **pre-warmed** for hot food and chilled for cold items before service.

Cutlery (The Flatware)

**Variety:** Forks, knives, spoons (soup, dessert, teaspoon). Ensure quality stainless steel, polished and spot-free.

**Placement:** Laid out in the order of use (**outside-in**) for plated service, or neatly bundled/wrapped for buffet.

Glassware & Stemware

**Variety:** Water glasses, wine glasses (red/white), champagne flutes, and specialized glasses for cocktails/mocktails. Avoid using plastic/disposable if possible.

**Check:** Must be sparkling clean, without water spots or streaks. Hold glasses by the stem or base.

Table Placement & Settings

**Standard:** Place the **show plate** (or dinner plate) in the center. Forks on the left, Knives and Spoons on the right. Glassware top-right.

**Napkin:** Use high-quality cloth napkins, neatly folded, placed on the plate or to the left of the forks.

Quantity & Backup Stock

**Calculation:** Order **1.5 to 2 times** the expected guest count for plates/cutlery to account for breaks, drops, and guests using multiple sets (especially for buffet).

**Crucial:** Ensure enough **tea/coffee cups and dessert spoons** for the last service phase.

Buffet Service Strategy

**Setup:** Place a **fresh stack of clean plates** at the *start* of the buffet line. Never allow guests to reuse their plates.

**Cutlery:** Best provided in a separate, easily accessible location *after* the food line, often wrapped in napkins.

Hygiene & Sanitization

**Standard:** All items must be thoroughly washed, sterilized, and handled with gloves during setup and transport.

**Storage:** Transport and store clean items in **covered containers** until the moment of placement to prevent dust or contamination.

Clearance & Dish Disposal

**System:** Implement a quick system for clearing tables and counters. Use bus tubs or dedicated carts that keep **food waste and dishes separate**.

**Staffing:** Adequate **busing staff** is required to ensure empty plates are removed swiftly, maintaining a clean look and clear space.
