Uniformed Security Guards

**Role:** High-visibility deterrent and initial response team. Used for patrolling the perimeter, securing entry/exit points, and stationary posts.

**Requirement:** Must be licensed, well-briefed, and easily identifiable (uniforms/vests).

Access Control & Ticketing

**Focus:** Managing the flow of attendees at main entry gates. Involves checking tickets, invitations, or accreditation passes.

**Staffing:** Requires dedicated staff at every entry point to prevent unauthorized access and minimize queues.

VIP & Personnel Protection

**Role:** Close protection for keynote speakers, celebrities, or the host's family. Usually involves discrete, plain-clothes personnel.

**Planning:** Requires pre-event route and venue mapping to secure movement paths and private areas.

Crowd Control & Flow Management

**Focus:** Directing large groups, especially near stages, food counters, and high-traffic areas. Preventing bottlenecks and aggressive behavior.

**Technique:** Use barriers (ropes, barricades) and clear verbal communication to maintain order.

Asset Protection (Equipment)

**Role:** Guarding valuable equipment like generators, expensive video/audio consoles, lighting desks, and sponsor booths.

**Placement:** Assign personnel to discreetly monitor the back-of-house (BOH) and tech areas where equipment is stored or operated.

Bag Checks & Frisking

**Protocol:** Mandatory checks for large-scale public events to prevent the entry of prohibited items (weapons, outside food/alcohol, banners).

**Requirement:** Must be clearly communicated to attendees. Requires separate male/female staff for personal frisking.

Pre-Event Briefing & Communication

**Crucial Step:** All security staff must attend a detailed briefing covering the event timeline, sensitive areas, chain of command, and specific security threats.

**Gear:** Ensure clear, operational communication via **two-way radios/walkie-talkies** for coordination.

Emergency Response Protocol

**Plan:** Define clear procedures for all emergencies: fire, medical incident, unauthorized entry, or mass evacuation.

**Liaison:** Establish a direct line of communication with local police, fire department, and ambulance services.
