Power Load Calculation (KVA)

**Key Step:** Accurately calculate the total **power consumption** of ALL equipment (lights, sound, displays, catering) to determine the size of the required backup system.

**Safety Margin:** Always factor in an additional **25-30% buffer** for safety and starting current.

Diesel Generator (DG) Set

**Primary Backup:** Essential for outdoor or high-power consumption events. Choose a **Silent/Acoustic** enclosure to minimize noise pollution near the venue.

**Consideration:** Must be installed away from the main venue with proper **earthing** and ventilation.

UPS (Uninterruptible Power Supply)

**Critical Use:** Provides **instant power** (zero downtime) for sensitive equipment like computers, audio mixers, and lighting consoles until the main generator starts.

**Benefit:** Prevents data loss and audio/video glitches during the critical few seconds of power switchover.

üîÅ

Transfer Switch (ATS/MTS)

**Function:** A switch that safely and efficiently moves the electrical load from the main grid source to the backup generator source when power fails.

**Importance:** Prevents dangerous **back-feeding** of power into the grid, protecting utility workers.

Power Distribution Boxes (PDBs)

**Core Requirement:** The central unit that takes the main power feed (from grid or DG) and splits it into multiple **individually fused circuits** (Audio, Lighting, Video).

**Advantage:** Isolates faults; if the light circuit trips, the audio and video systems remain running.

Electrical Safety & Earthing

**Priority:** All main equipment and distribution boxes must have proper **Earthing (Grounding)** to prevent shock hazards and equipment damage.

**Check:** Use **RCDs (Residual Current Devices)** on sensitive circuits for instantaneous power cut-off in case of leakage.

Fuel & Run Time Planning

**Logistics:** Ensure the DG set has enough fuel for the **entire duration of the event plus a 25% reserve**. Arrange for on-site fuel management if the event is multi-day.

**Testing:** A **load bank test** must be performed on the DG set before installation to confirm its output capacity.

Cable Management & Protection

**Safety:** All cables must be correctly rated and protected with **cable ramps/trays** where they cross public pathways. Never run power cables through waterlogged areas.

**Best Practice:** Keep power and audio/video cables **physically separate** to prevent audio hum/interference.
