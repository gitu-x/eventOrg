Detailed Inventory & Checklist

**Crucial:** Create a master list of **every single item** coming into the venue (decor, sound gear, furniture, catering boxes). Use labels and numbering for easy identification.

**Goal:** Prevents loss, damage, and ensures nothing is forgotten during teardown.

Venue Access Points & Docks

**Planning:** Identify the **best loading dock, cargo elevator, or ramp** for large deliveries. Confirm size and weight limits for elevators/entrances.

**Coordination:** Communicate specific delivery slots to all vendors to prevent bottlenecks at the dock.

Movement Schedule (In & Out)

**Timeline:** Create a **Staggered Setup Schedule** (e.g., Heavy equipment first, then Decor, then Catering). Reverse the schedule exactly for teardown.

**Safety:** Do not allow multiple vendors to load/unload heavy items simultaneously in the same narrow area.

Handling Manpower & Equipment

**Staffing:** Assign dedicated **BOH (Back of House) Logistics Staff** to manage receiving and dispatching goods.

**Tools:** Ensure availability of necessary equipment: **Pallet jacks, trollies, heavy-duty hand trucks**, and reliable communication (radios).

Vehicle Registration & Parking

**Protocol:** Pre-register all vendor vehicles (trucks, vans) and provide them with **temporary access passes** for loading zones and staging areas.

**Duration:** Enforce strict time limits (e.g., 30 minutes) for vehicles at the loading dock to keep the line moving.

Venue Protection & Safety

**Mandatory:** Use **protective mats, plywood sheets, or cardboard** on carpets and sensitive flooring along all major movement routes to prevent damage from heavy traffic.

**Security:** Ensure all high-value items are under watch or immediately secured upon arrival.

Back of House (BOH) Staging Area

**Planning:** Designate a secure, organized area for temporary storage and unpacking/packing. Use **color-coded zones** for different departments (A/V, Decor, F&B).

**Tidiness:** Maintain a clear walkway in the BOH area at all times for staff safety.

Reverse Logistics (Teardown)

**Speed:** Teardown is usually time-critical. The process must be **faster and more structured** than the setup. All boxes and crates must be pre-labeled.

**Final Check:** Do a final "sweep" check of the venue using the initial inventory list before the last vendor leaves.
