Proscenium Stage (End Stage)

**Focus:** Classic "picture frame" stage where the audience faces one side. Great for large theatrical, musical, or corporate presentations requiring a backdrop.

**Best For:** Concerts, product launches with large screens, formal addresses.

Thrust Stage

**Focus:** The stage "thrusts" into the audience, who sit on three sides. Increases intimacy and provides multiple viewing angles.

**Best For:** Fashion shows, interactive performances, debates, and speaker Q&A sessions.

Arena / In the Round

**Focus:** The stage is central, with the audience surrounding it on all four sides (360 degrees). Creates the most intense intimacy and energy.

**Best For:** Wrestling, central music performances (e.g., boxing/sports events), large experiential events.

Platform Stage

**Focus:** A simple, raised area without elaborate curtains or backdrops. Used primarily to ensure visibility above the crowd.

**Best For:** Speeches in a banquet hall, DJ setups, small press conferences, quick awards ceremonies.

Catwalk / Runway Stage

**Focus:** A long, narrow stage extending into the audience, often used for display or movement. Maximizes interaction and visual exposure.

**Best For:** Fashion shows, product demonstrations, dance performances, bridal entry at weddings.

Modular Stage

**Focus:** Sections of staging that can be easily rearranged to fit different layouts (e.g., split stage, tiered stage). Highly flexible and adaptable.

**Best For:** Events with multiple segments (e.g., a corporate event that needs a stage for a speaker, then a platform for a band).

Tiered Stage (Multilevel)

**Focus:** A stage with multiple height levels (steps or platforms). Adds visual depth and allows many performers to be seen simultaneously.

**Best For:** Large choral performances, band setups with drums/keyboards, group panel discussions, photo opportunities.

Corner Stage

**Focus:** Placed in the corner of a room, saving floor space while still providing a focal point. Utilizes the room's natural layout.

**Best For:** Lounge events, small acoustic sets, comedy acts, where the stage is a background element.
